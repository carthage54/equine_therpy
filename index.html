Equine Therapy

As many as 500,000 veterans with Post Traumatic Stress are receiving no therapy whatsoever today.

What is Equine Therapy? Horses actually have the same mental behaviors that a veteran with PTSD has, and this helps attract the veteran to the horse. Horses are hyper alert to their surrounding and hyper reactive to potential threats. They like to be in a herd where all are watching for threat dangers. Not only do they watch their surroundings, but they are extremely good at “reading” anxiety in the humans around them as another indicator of threats in the area. They can detect fear, anxiety and anger in humans that no other human or animal can.

This ability to detect anxiety in humans is what allows the horse to “teach” veterans how they can self-manage their internal anxiety levels. When the veteran is working with their horse and has high anxiety, the horse is very jumpy because they perceive that a threat must be in the area. Jumpy, reactive horses are very hard to work with. So the veteran is encouraged to calm their anxiety using various techniques.

Our Program

Our program consists of three classes, each meets once a week for 8 weeks with each class building on the previous one. In "Introduction to Horsemanship" the veteran gets to know his horse while learning how to care for him. In the second class, "Introduction to Riding", the veteran learns how to ride his horse thus strengthening the bond between them. The third class, "Introduction to Mountain Obstacles" the veteran and horse learn to navigate thru obstacles strengthening the trust between them.
